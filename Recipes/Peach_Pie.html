Peach Pie

Ingredients:

  • 8-10 ripe peaches and/or nectarines
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar (packed)
  • 2 tablespoons sugar, plus 1 tablespoon to sprinkle over crust
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground nutmeg
  • 6-8 whole cloves
  • 1-2 tablespoons instant tapioca
  • A pinch of salt
  • 2 9-inch pie crusts (see Norma's Pie Crust for recipe)
  • 1 egg, beaten

Preparation:

  1. Peel and slice peaches into a large bowl. Add sugars and lemonjuice and toss gently. Cover and place the bowl in the refrigerator and let sit for about 1 hour.
  2. After an hour, take the bowl out of the fridge and strain the peach juice into a small saucepan. Bring the juice to a simmer and add nutmeg, cloves, salt, and tapioca. Stirring constantly, continue to simmer the juice for about 3 minutes, or until it begins to thicken.
  3. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees Farenheight. Take the prepared pie crusts from the fridge and flour a rolling-surface and rolling pin. Roll one pie crust and place into a pie dish. Trim off the excess dough from around the edges of the dish and add the sliced peaches.
  4. Roll the second pie crust. Making sure to remove the cloves beforehand, pour the juice sauce over the peach filling and gently place the top crust over the peaches. Press the sides of the crust together, trim off any excess dough, brush with the egg, and sprinkle with the reserved sugar.
  5. Place the pie in the preheated oven. Bake the pie at 400 degrees for around 30 minutes, or until the crust has started to brown, after which point you must turn the pie 180 degrees and turn down the temperature of the oven to around 300 degrees. Bake until the entire crust is golden brown (15-20 minutes).
  6. Remove the pie from the oven and place on a rack to cool. Let cool slightly before serving.
