Oatmeal-Raisin Granola Bars

Ingredients:

Makes 16 bars

  • 1 cup whole old-fashioned rolled oats
  • 1 cup quick-cooking oatmeal
  • 1 cup rice krispies
  • 1/4 cup raisins
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 plus 3 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon almond extract
  • A little cooking oil

Preparation:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit, or 175 degrees Celcius.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the old-fashioned oats, oatmeal, rice krispies, raisins, and cinnamon.
  3. Bring 1/2 a cup of honey, maple syrup, and brown sugar to a simmer in a small saucepan, stirring constantly to combine. Once simmering, turn off the heat and add the vanilla and almond extracts. Pour the syrup mixture over the dry ingredients and stir until sticky and well-coated.
  4. Pour the contents of the large bowl into a parchment-lined baking pan. Coat the bottom of a flat metal kitchen utensil, such as a measuring cup, with a bit of cooking oil to prevent it from sticking, and use it to spread and press the mixture down evenly into the pan. Press very firmly, or the bars will crumble once they have come out of the oven. Before baking, quickly drizzle the top with about 3 more tablespoons of honey to make sure the bars stick together.
  5. Place the pan into the oven and bake for 15 to 20 minutes, or until the top of the granola has just started to turn golden. Make sure not to over-bake or the bars will dry out and become granola. At this point, take the granola out of the oven and let it cool completely in the pan before cutting into 16 equally-sized bars.
  6. Note: If the granola bars crumble during cutting, simply break up the rest, turning them into cinnamon raisin granola.
