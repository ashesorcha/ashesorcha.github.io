Marion's Irish Scones

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound flour
  • A pinch of salt
  • 3 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 ounce sugar
  • 3 ounces cold cubed butter
  • 2 eggs
  • 7 fluid ounces cold milk
  • Optional additions: Berries (raspberries, blueberries, etc.), nuts, zest, chocolate...

Variations & Flavors:

  • Blueberry Scones
  • Raspberry Scones
  • Orange Cranberry Scones
  • Lemon Scones
  • Cranberry White Chocolate Scones
  • Apple Cinnamon Scones
  • ...

Preparation:

  1. Preheat the oven to around 390 degrees Farenheit or 200 degrees Celcius if you are planning to bake the scones right away.
  2. Sieve flour, salt, and baking powder into a large bowl and stir in the sugar.
  3. Break and blend in the butter by hand until the mixture resembles bread crumbs.
  4. Beat the eggs into the milk. Form a well in the dry ingredients and pour the wet mixture into the well, retaining a little for the egg wash.
  5. Now is the time to add any additional flavoring ingredients to the scones (e.g. berries, nuts, etc). Using your hands, fold the elements together just to combine into a soft dough. Do not overmix the dough, as overmixing will make the scones dense and loose their flakiness.
  6. On a floured surface, roll or pat the dough to a one inch thickness. Cut the dough into similar-sized portions with a knife or cookie cutter.
  7. One can choose either to bake the scones immediately or freeze the cut dough for later use. To bake, place the scones on a lined baking sheet and bake in the oven for around 20-30 minutes, or until the tops have turned golden brown, cracks have appeared on the sides, and the dough is firm but springs back when touched.
  8. To freeze, place the unbaked dough cut-outs into a lined container and store in the freezer until ready to bake. When wanted again, preheat the oven, take the scones out of the freezer, place them on a lined baking sheet to defrost for around 15 minutes, and bake according to previous directions.
  9. To serve, pair these scones with butter, cream, and jam. They can also be eaten with berries, nuts, and whatever else you choose.
